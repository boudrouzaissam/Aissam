Why We Keep Old Things

By Aissam Boudrouz · Everyday Economics

We often keep old objects even when we no longer use them. Clothes, books, devices, documents, and memories stay with us because they once had value.

In economics, this can be connected to the sunk cost fallacy.

The sunk cost fallacy

A sunk cost is a cost that has already been paid and cannot be recovered. Rationally, it should not determine our future decisions. But emotionally, it often does.

We keep things because we paid for them, waited for them, worked for them, or attached memories to them.

The lesson

Not every old thing should be thrown away. Some objects have emotional value. But the sunk cost fallacy reminds us to ask a simple question: would I choose this again today?

Sometimes, letting go is not a loss. It is a correction of a past decision.
